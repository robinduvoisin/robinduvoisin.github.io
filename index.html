I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Neural Systems and Computation, offered jointly by the University of Zurich (UZH) and the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich (ETHZ), within the Neuroinformatics Department. Throughout my academic journey, I've explored neuroscience from a top-down perspective, commencing with an introduction to EEG recording and progressing to LFP analysis. A pivotal experience that redirected my interest appeared during a lecture on Bioelectronics and Biosensors, led by Professors Vörös, Fatih, and Dr. W. von der Behrens. This course introduced me to Hodgkin-Huxley models and to the intricate physics involved in neuron recording. Following this lecture, I discovered that while my previous experiences with neural analysis had yielded intriguing results, I am now driven to explore more the dynamics behind neural computations. Whether through neural dynamics and stability analysis or as interconnected networks where memories or feature extractions emerge.
