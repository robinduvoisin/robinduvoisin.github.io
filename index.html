I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Neural Systems and Computation, jointly offered by the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Zurich (ETHZ) and the University of Zurich (UZH) within the Neuroinformatics Department. My academic journey has been rooted in data-driven neuroscience, starting with EEG recording and advancing to local field potential (LFP) analysis. A pivotal experience occurred during the Bioelectronics and Biosensors course taught by Professors Vörös, Fatih, and W. von der Behrens. I particularly enjoyed this lecture because it struck a perfect balance between analyzing neural signals and modeling the physics underlying the principles of neuron recording. I realized I wanted to deepen my theoretical background in areas such as Complex Networks, Information Theory, and Models of Computation to better explore the dynamics behind neural computation.
