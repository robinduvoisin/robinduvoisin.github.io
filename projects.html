Academics Projects

Below, you can find the various projects I had the pleasure of working on during my Bachelor's and Master's studies.

Birdsong Group

During a 3-month internship,I served as a research assistant in Prof. Hahnloser's Birdsong Group under the supervision of Corinna Lorenz. In this role, I conducted an analysis of neuroelectrophysiological data, including local field potentials and spiking activity. I was able to prove that the dynamics of signals from two distinct brain areas involved in song diversity generation tend to synchronize during sleep stages, revealing a potential channel of communication for error correction and song diversity adaptation. Additionally, I applied a similar analysis to a bird performing a pitch-learning task to investigate the presence of phase-lock LFP signals, indicative of learning. This project enhanced my skills in signal processing and big data analysis, as well as offering me a solid introduction to spiking activity and communication between populations of neurons.

Uncovering Sleep Modulation Effects through EEG signals

I had the opportunity to conduct my bachelor's project (grade 6) with the Neural Control of Movement Lab at ETH Zurich, under the supervision of Dr. Lustenberger and Manuel C. Dominguez. My project was part of the research for the Sleeploop program jointly conducted by UZH and ETH, and it comprised two main components. In the first part, I designed a protocol for an experiment aimed at evaluating the impact of pre-sleep arousal levels on memory consolidation. The second part involved processing EEG data obtained from a finger-tapping task. I conducted an analysis of the dynamics of power spectral densities and correlated them with indicators of neuronal fatigue.
